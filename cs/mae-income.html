Mortgage Application Experience

Redesigning Barclays’ broker mortgage application journey to simplify income input and reduce policy errors.

Content design UX writing Financial services

Context: Income is central to a mortgage application. Overtime, commission and bonuses can only be counted if they meet specific lending criteria.

Role: I led a small content design team, working directly in Figma alongside designers and policy experts.

Outcome: Early designs placed too much weight on brokers’ own knowledge and interpretation, which testing showed created unnecessary cognitive load. Iteration focused on simplifying the flow, clarifying policy guidance and moving complex calculations into the system – making the process faster and less error-prone.

1. Original design

A fragmented content structure made this screen hard to understand, and the placeholder copy didn’t help. More critically, it lacked key policy information. Whether a bonus is treated as ‘annual’ or ‘monthly’ depends on how often it’s paid – not always every year or month. Without clear guidance, brokers made errors, and underwriters had to step in.

Why it matters: Errors in this income section were common and caused delays to lending decisions.

2. Content-first improvements

The MVP approach was to fix pain points through content. After reviewing policy on overtime, commission, bonuses and payslips, I rewrote the guidance, simplified the structure and removed irrelevant detail.

Why it matters: The screen now aligned with policy requirements and read more clearly, but it still relied on long text and manual calculations by the broker.

3. Structural simplification

Adding a ‘payment frequency’ question let us split guidance by scenario and show only what applied. This reduced clutter and improved flow, but brokers still had to calculate this income manually – not ideal in a digital journey.

Why it matters: Content changes alone could only go so far. Real simplification needed a technical solution.

4. Final design

Working with the solutions architect, we confirmed the system could calculate income automatically. We tested options and chose to ask for payslip totals rather than every individual figure – keeping data entry manageable.

Why it matters: The guidance was reduced to a few short prompts about evidence. Automating the maths cut user errors and sped up applications.

Outcomes and impact

Result: Part of a wider redesign that cut application times by about three-quarters, reduced underwriter referrals for input errors, and significantly improved broker satisfaction.

The project showed how aligning content design, policy logic and system behaviour can reduce complexity at scale – replacing cognitive load with confidence.
